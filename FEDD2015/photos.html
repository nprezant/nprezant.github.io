These pictures are from our first night physically building something.

  • Here is our first hovercraft. We decided that we must actually "do," because up until this point we had only talked about theoretical things we were going to build.
  • We discovered that cutting holes in the skirt actually does increase lift. Even so, it is always scary to cut the skirt.
  • In that study room A of D.H. Hill, we did have some good times.
  • This idea was solid, but we gave up on it in pursuit of other things before really testing it out.

Next we said, well we could probably just make one a lot bigger.

  • Yeah that didn't work out very well.

Since that wouldn't even lift itself with the moter/propellor power we had, we decided to make an assortment of smaller bases with the same type of bag skirt and try our luck with those.

  • Be there or be square
  • But don't be a circle either.

One of these trials (not pictured), was a pentagon shape, and worked best with the propeller we had. So, we took that base and stuck the same vanilla-scented bag skirt on it.

  • Our pre-FEDD trial run ready hovercraft.
  • In this picture you can also see our "controller."

From there, we went to the pre-FEDD testing event. While there, we were told to try to use more of a wall-skirt design. So, we got some firmer material and tried many different hole shapes and sizes.

  • The first one, bound to be a goober.
  • We were scared to cut the holes too big, but we may have aired on the side of too small for this guy.
  • This skirt worked pretty well, and is close to the design we ended up sticking with.

      • We then combined the newer skirt with our pentagonal hovercraft to come up with this as our final design!
